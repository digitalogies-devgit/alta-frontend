How Much Do Servers Really Cost? A Deep Dive into Server Investment

May 23, 2024
Share:

If you run a business today, or manage the IT, chances are you rely on servers in some capacity. But servers can be crazy expensive, whether on prem or cloud-based. You want to provide your business with the infrastructure it needs, but the potential costs give you pause.

Between opaque pricing, complex licensing, and ongoing maintenance fees, many companies feel perplexed when budgeting for new servers. And there is also the sustainability factor to consider. With e-waste a growing global crisis, is there a way to procure reliable servers while also extending the lifecycle of already manufactured hardware? Can you limit your Scope3 Emissions that are creeping into reporting requirements?

This conundrum of balancing costs, sustainability, and performance might be leaving you frustrated and unsure of the best path forward. You need help to justify the investment required amidst constrained budgets and uncertainty about their options.

The good news is there are solutions if you know where to look. This guide will cut through the haze surrounding server expenses and empower you to make smart infrastructure decisions for your business. 

We'll unpack the true costs involved, compare buying vs. renting, and reveal how refurbished servers can provide premium quality at a fraction of the price of new models. You'll gain clarity on server spending and the confidence to architect an IT environment that bolsters your operations rather than draining your resources. 

Let’s dive in!

The Real Cost of Servers: Beyond the Price Tag

When considering the cost of a server, it's crucial to look beyond the initial price tag. Here's what you need to factor in:

  • Hardware Expenses: This includes the server itself, along with any additional components such as memory, disk, GPUs, network cards or attached storage.
  • Operational Costs: Think electricity, cooling, connectivity, and the space your server occupies. These can add up, especially if you're running multiple servers 24/7.
  • Maintenance and Support:  Servers need regular updates and occasional repairs. Factor in these ongoing costs to keep your server at peak performance.
  • Software Licenses:  Operating systems and applications aren't always free. Remember to budget for any software your server will need.

Renting vs. Buying Servers: What's Best for Your Bottom Line?

Renting servers, often through cloud services, offers flexibility and scalability. It's a good match for businesses with variable demands or those looking to avoid upfront investments. However, buying on-prem might be more cost-effective if you need a server for the long haul, especially if you consider refurbished options.

Companies in the midst of a move or short-term project, may also be great candidates for a short-term rental. Many companies rent refurbished servers for 1 to 11 months for these types of projects.
